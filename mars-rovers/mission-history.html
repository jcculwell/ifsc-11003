Detailed Mission Timeline

Explore the key milestones and missions in NASA's Mars Exploration Program, charting humanity's journey across the Red Planet.

July 4, 1997 (Landing)

Sojourner: The First Rover

Launched as part of the Mars Pathfinder mission, Sojourner was the first wheeled vehicle to explore the surface of Mars. It operated for 83 sols (Martian days), proving the concept of rovers and inspiring future designs.

January 2004 (Landing)

Spirit and Opportunity (MER)

The twin rovers, Spirit and Opportunity, were designed for 90-sol missions to search for signs of past water activity. Spirit operated for six years, while the remarkably resilient Opportunity operated for nearly 15 years, covering 45 km.

August 6, 2012 (Landing)

Curiosity: Martian Climate and Geology

The Mars Science Laboratory (MSL) mission delivered Curiosity, a car-sized rover, to Gale Crater. Its primary goal is to assess whether Mars ever had an environment capable of supporting microbial life. It continues its mission today.

February 18, 2021 (Landing)

Perseverance: Seeking Ancient Life

Landing in Jezero Crater, Perseverance is searching for signs of ancient microbial life and is collecting core samples for eventual return to Earth. It also deployed the Ingenuity helicopter, the first powered aircraft on another planet.

Late 2020s (Planned)

Mars Sample Return Mission

A complex, multi-mission effort to bring the samples collected by Perseverance back to Earth for detailed study by scientists worldwide, fulfilling a high-priority goal of planetary science.
