Common Questions

Why are rovers sent to Mars?

Rovers allow scientists to explore the Martian surface directly, study rocks, search for evidence of past microbial life, and gather crucial data to prepare for future human exploration. They act as robotic field geologists, collecting samples and measurements that orbiters cannot.

How do rovers communicate with Earth?

They primarily use the network of NASA orbiters (like the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter or MAVEN) as relays. The rover sends a signal to the closer orbiter, which then sends the data back to Earth. This method requires significantly less power than sending a direct signal across the vast distance.

How long do missions last?

Most rovers are designed for short primary missions (Spirit and Opportunity were designed for 90 Martian days, or sols). However, many missions exceed their lifespan dramatically, especially the nuclear-powered rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance, which are still active and generating science far beyond their initial projections.

What is the Ingenuity helicopter?

Ingenuity was a small, experimental helicopter that accompanied the Perseverance rover. It successfully demonstrated the first powered, controlled flight on another planet, proving that aerial reconnaissance and exploration is possible in the thin Martian atmosphere. It completed dozens of flights before retiring.
